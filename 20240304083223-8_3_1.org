#+title: 8.3.1
Let $H \subseteq G \subseteq \bar{G}$ be inclusions of hyperplanes and $\bar{o} \in
\bar{G} \setminus G$. We consider another hyperplane $G'$ of $\bar{G}$ with $G
\cap G' = H$ and $\bar{o} \not \in G'$. The map $\pi \coloneqq
\operatorname{pr}_{G, G'}^{\bar{o}}$ (cf. 6.6.9) is an isomorphism $G \to G'$.
For any $\bar{a} \in \bar{G}$ we define
$$
\Phi_{\bar{a}} \coloneqq \operatorname{pr}_{G', G}^{\bar{a}} \circ
\operatorname{pr}_{G,G'}^{\bar{o}} = \operatorname{pr}_{G', G}^{\bar{a}} \circ \pi
$$
Then one has $\Phi_{\bar{a}} \in \mathscr{E}_{H}$, i.e. one gets a map $\Phi :
\bar{G} \to \mathscr{E}_{H}$. This map $\Phi$ is bijective. Moreover, for
$\bar{a} \not = \bar{o}$ the point $\operatorname{pr}_{G}^{\bar{o}}(\bar{a})$
is the center of $\Phi_{\bar{a}}$ (cf. 6.6.8 for $\operatorname{pr}_{G}^{\bar{o}}$).
